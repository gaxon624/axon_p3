CRIMINAL RECORDS

This game can be played online or with a group of friends! The left side contains information for the in-person group version, or you can play alone on the right side! Refresh the page to change the random information given about the criminal!

Participants will team up and sit in a circle to create a cast of criminal characters that are documentary worthy. After they set up their character’s “criminal record”, there are no laws or limits on what the participants can draw or write about them!

  • 3-5 Participants
  • Markers and regular sized printer paper
  • 8-12 minutes
  • At the start of the activity, each participant will sit in a circle and pick a different color from each other and use only the corresponding marker for the rest of the activity. Then they will spend the first 3 minutes drawing a portrait of a person/face on the top right corner in a box on a piece of letter sized paper. They can draw anything they want as long as it has a face and is drawn in a box in the top right corner of the page. (The box can vary in size but leave room to write on the left side of the page)
  • If the three minutes are up, then each participant will spend the next minute writing six evenly spaced, underlined labels on the left side of the page for Name, Age, height and weight (together as one label), Bio (1-2 sentence biography), Crimes, and Sentence
  • If the one minute is up and each person has written the labels correctly on the page, then they will pass their paper to the person to the left of them
  • If papers are passed, then each participant will write for one label of their choosing on the document they receive under their chosen header (either name, age, height and weight, bio, crimes, or sentence) Note: Anything can be written in any label, but it should make sense for the given label (example: don’t put someone’s age on the name label)
  • If each person has written one of the labels on the document they’ve been given, then they will pass the document to the person on their left and write for a different label on the new document they receive (that hasn’t been written for already)
  • If every label on each document gets written for, then each participant may spend one minute to make edits to the portrait of the criminal on the document they currently have (edits to the portrait can be whatever the participants want, with the written content for the labels as potential inspiration)
  • If every label on each document gets written for and the one-minute timer for portrait edits is up, then each person will read and show the document they currently have and read it aloud and discuss it (and hopefully laugh) with the other participants.
